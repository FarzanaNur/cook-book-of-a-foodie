Mixed Chow-mein

Ingredients:

Chow-mein Noodles, Boneless Chicken, Egg (optional), Choice of Vegetable, Mushroom (optional), Black Paper, Salt, Soya Sauce, Butter/Oil, Oregano (optional), Chilli-flex.

Process:

First, boil water, and then add the noodles along with a tip of oil and a pinch of salt; the salt will help season the noodles and prevent them from sticking together. Next, add butter to a pan and, over medium heat, scramble the egg with a bit of salt and pepper. Once the egg is done, add the chicken, mushrooms, and vegetables, seasoning with salt and pepper according to taste. After a few minutes, stir in the sauce mixture (dark soy sauce, light soy sauce, oyster sauce, Worcestershire sauce [optional], tomato sauce, and sugar to taste), and allow it to simmer for a few minutes. If the mixture becomes too thick, you can add a little of the noodle cooking water. Meanwhile, check if the noodles are 90% cooked; if they are, drain them and let them cool. Remember, never overcook the noodles! Finally, add the noodles to the chicken and vegetable mixture, toss everything together, and adjust the seasoning with salt, pepper, and chili flakes. Your dish is now ready to enjoy!
