Mixed Chow-mein

Ingredients:

Chow-mein Noodles, Boneless Chicken, Egg (optional), Choice of Vegetable, Mushroom (optional), Black Paper, Salt, Soya Sauce, Butter/Oil, Oregano (optional), Chilli-flex.

Process:

First, boil water, and then add the noodles along with a tip of oil and a pinch of salt; the salt will help season the noodles and prevent them from sticking together. Next, add butter to a pan and, over medium heat, scramble the egg with a bit of salt and pepper. Once the egg is done, add the chicken, mushrooms, and vegetables, seasoning with salt and pepper according to taste. After a few minutes, stir in the sauce mixture (dark soy sauce, light soy sauce, oyster sauce, Worcestershire sauce [optional], tomato sauce, and sugar to taste), and allow it to simmer for a few minutes. If the mixture becomes too thick, you can add a little of the noodle cooking water. Meanwhile, check if the noodles are 90% cooked; if they are, drain them and let them cool. Remember, never overcook the noodles! Finally, add the noodles to the chicken and vegetable mixture, toss everything together, and adjust the seasoning with salt, pepper, and chili flakes. Your dish is now ready to enjoy!

Chicken Mo-Mo

Ingredients:

For Filling: Boneless chicken, Ginger-Garlic paste/powder,Soya sauce,Choice of Vegetables (for this recipe i used carrots only),salt,paper.
For dough:All purpose flour,salt,oil.
For Sauce:Chili flex, Tamarind,Water,Oil.

Process:

For the momos, start by preparing the filling using finely chopped boneless chicken, ginger-garlic paste or powder, soy sauce, finely grated carrots (or any other vegetables of your choice), salt, and pepper. Mix everything well and set aside. For the dough, combine all-purpose flour with a pinch of salt, a little oil, and enough water to form a smooth dough, then let it rest for about 30 minutes. Roll out the dough into small circles, place a spoonful of the chicken and vegetable filling in the center, and fold the edges together to seal each momo. Steam the momos for 10-15 minutes until the dough is cooked through. For the sauce, mix chili flakes, tamarind, water, and oil, then heat it until well combined. Serve the momos hot with the prepared sauce.

Tuna Kebab

Ingredients:

Canned tuna, Choice of Vegetables (i used carrots), Mashed Potato, Onion, Green Chili, Salt, Black pepper, Rice-flour or Corn-flour.
You can replace Tuna with your choice of fish.Make sure to boil it beforehand if it's raw.

Process:

To prepare delicious tuna kababs, start by combining drained canned tuna, grated carrot, mashed boiled potato, finely chopped onion, and green chilies in a mixing bowl. Season the mixture generously with salt and black pepper, ensuring all flavors are well blended. Add 2-3 tablespoons of rice flour or corn flour to bind the mixture, mixing thoroughly until it holds together firmly. Shape the mixture into evenly sized patties, then heat oil in a pan over medium heat. Shallow fry the kababs for 3-4 minutes on each side, or until they turn golden brown. Serve hot with your favorite chutney or ketchup for a flavorful snack.
